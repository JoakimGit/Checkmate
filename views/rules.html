Rules of chess

Chess is a two-player board game where each player has 16 pieces total. Two rooks, two knights, 2 bishops, a king, a queen and 8 pawns. Each piece moves in its own way.

The object of the game is to checkmate the opponent's king, but games often end in another way.

A player who knows their position is lost will often choose to resign. There are also several ways for a game to end in a draw.

The player with the white pieces has the first move. Players then alternate moves. You can't skip a move and the game continues until an end has been reached.

Starting position

The king moves 1 square in any direction. He can also castle once during the game, moving two squares towards a rook.

The rook moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically. It also moves to the other side of the king when castling.

The bishop moves any number of squares diagonally.

The queen moves any number of squares, horizontally, vertically or diagonally.

The knight moves in an L shape. Two squares horizontally and 1 square vertically, or vice-versa. It can't be blocked.

The pawn moves 1 square forward, or two from its original position. It can also capture 1 square diagonally. Upon reaching the other end of the board, it promotes into another piece.

A king is in check when it is under attack by one or more enemy pieces. It is not possible to make a move that would place one's own king in check.

The game is automatically a draw if the player to move is not in check and has no legal move. This is called stalemate.
